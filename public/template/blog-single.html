5 Signs Your Refrigerator Is About to Break Down

  1. Swift Fix Team
  2. May 8, 2025
  3. Refrigerator

Your refrigerator runs 24 hours a day, 7 days a week — and like any hard-working appliance, it will eventually show signs of wear. The key is knowing what to look for before a small problem turns into a full breakdown, a spoiled fridge full of food, and an emergency repair call.

Most refrigerator failures don't happen suddenly. They come with warning signs that are easy to miss if you don't know what you're looking at. In this article, we walk through the five most common indicators that your refrigerator needs professional attention — and what you should do about each one.

1. The Motor Runs Constantly — or Not at All

A healthy refrigerator cycles on and off throughout the day. If you notice the compressor is running non-stop, or conversely, never seems to kick on, something is off. A constantly running motor is often a sign of a failing compressor, dirty condenser coils, or a faulty thermostat. Left unchecked, it can cause the motor to burn out entirely — a far more expensive repair.

2. Food Is Spoiling Faster Than Normal

If your milk is going bad ahead of schedule or your leftovers seem to spoil faster than they should, your refrigerator may not be maintaining the right temperature. The ideal fridge temperature is between 35°F and 38°F (1.7°C–3.3°C). Use a simple thermometer to check — if your unit is running warmer, the issue could be a failing evaporator fan, a refrigerant leak, or a damaged door seal.

"A refrigerator that can't hold temperature isn't just an inconvenience — it's a food safety risk. When in doubt, call a technician before the problem gets worse."

3. Excessive Frost or Ice Buildup in the Freezer

A thin layer of frost is normal, but thick ice accumulation — especially around the freezer walls or on stored food — is a red flag. This typically points to a problem with the defrost system: a faulty defrost timer, heater, or thermostat. Over time, heavy ice buildup restricts airflow and forces the compressor to work harder, accelerating wear on the entire unit.

4. Unusual Noises

Refrigerators make some noise — a gentle hum is perfectly normal. But loud clicking, rattling, buzzing, or knocking sounds are not. A clicking noise near startup or shutdown can indicate a failing start relay on the compressor. Rattling may mean a loose drain pan or a fan blade hitting accumulated ice. Buzzing can be a sign of a defective water inlet valve if your unit has an ice maker.

5. Water Pooling Under or Inside the Fridge

Finding water on the floor near your refrigerator — or puddles inside the compartment — signals a drainage or seal problem. A clogged defrost drain is the most common culprit: when the drain tube freezes over or becomes blocked, melting defrost water has nowhere to go. A worn or cracked door seal can also allow warm air in, causing excess condensation that eventually pools at the bottom.

What to Do Next

If you're noticing one or more of these signs, the best move is to act early. Minor refrigerator repairs — replacing a door seal, clearing a defrost drain, or swapping a start relay — are relatively inexpensive when caught early. Waiting until the compressor fails or the unit stops cooling entirely almost always means a bigger bill, or a full replacement.

  • Check your refrigerator's temperature with a standalone thermometer — don't rely on the built-in display alone
  • Keep condenser coils clean (located underneath or behind the unit) — dirty coils are a leading cause of overheating
  • Inspect door seals every few months by closing the door on a piece of paper — if it pulls out easily, the seal needs replacing
  • Listen for unusual sounds during the compressor startup cycle, typically every 10–15 minutes
  • If food is spoiling early or the freezer is icing over, schedule a diagnostic before the problem escalates
